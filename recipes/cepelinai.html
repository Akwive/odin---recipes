♡ Cepelinai ♡

==============================================================================================================================

==============================================================================================================================

The national dish of Lithuania, potato-meat dumplings, or in Lithuanian - cepelinai, are hearty and rich dumplings that take some time to make, but they are well worth the effort.

Don't be intimidated by the recipe as it has a few steps in order to make each component, including the filling, the dumpling, and the gravy.

==============================================================================================================================

==============================================================================================================================

~Ingredients~

For the meat filling:

  • 1 pound ground pork, or an equal mix of pork and beef
  • 1 medium onion, peeled and finely chopped
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 large egg, beaten

For the dumplings:

  • 1 tablespoon freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 10 large potatoes, peeled and finely grated
  • 1 medium onion, finely grated
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons kosher salt, more as needed
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch

For the Gravy:

  • 1/2 pound bacon, diced
  • 1 large onion, coarsely chopped
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 to 2 tablespoons milk, as needed

~Preparation steps~

  1. In a large bowl, mix well the meat, onion, salt, pepper and the egg.
  2. Cover with a plastic wrap and leave it to refrigerate.
  3. Grate the potatoes, and add lemon juice so the potatoes don't turn brown.
  4. Place them in a fine-mesh cheesecloth or cotton dishtowel, and twist over a large bowl to get rid of the excess water.
  5. Reserve the potato starch at the bottom of the bowl.
  6. PLace the raw potatoes in the bowl with potato starch, add onion, salt, and mix well.
  7. Start to boil the water

Form the dumplings:

  1. Take about 1 cup of dumpling mixture and pat it flat in the palm of the hand.
  2. Place 1/4 cup or more of meat mixture in the center.
  3. using slightly dampened hands, fold the potato mixture around the meat into a football shape, sealing well.

Cook the dumplings:

  1. Carefully lower dumplings nto salted, boiling water to which 1 tablespoon of cornstarch has been added.
  2. Make sure water returns to the boil; reduce the heat to low and continue simmering until the filling is cooked through, 20 to 25 minutes.
  3. Now prepare the gravy (instructions down below)
  4. Remove dumplings with a slotted spoon or strainer, drain briefly and place on a heated platter.

Make the gravy:

  1. In a medium skillet, fry the bacon and chopped onion until tender.
  2. Drain and combine with sour cream and black pepper. Thin with 1 to 2 tablespoons milk if necessary.
  3. Ladle the gravy over the dumplings or pass the gravy at the table.

Enjoy!
